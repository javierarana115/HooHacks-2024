Water Conservation

Summary

Water conservation refers to the practice of using water efficiently to reduce unnecessary water usage. It's essential
for maintaining water availability and is critical in areas prone to drought or where freshwater resources are scarce.
Water conservation can be practiced in various settings, including households, businesses, agriculture, and industry.
Implementing water-saving techniques not only helps preserve our precious water resources but also reduces the
energy required to process and deliver water, thereby reducing pollution and conserving energy.

Negative Impact on Aquatic Ecosystems

Water conservation efforts that significantly reduce the amount of water returned to natural waterways can negatively
impact aquatic ecosystems. Lower river flows can harm fish populations, reduce biodiversity, and alter the natural
balance.
